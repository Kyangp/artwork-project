I — The Artwork

A million people.
One artwork.

One Million Marks is a permanent collaborative artwork made from one million individual decisions. Each participant places one small mark. Once placed, it cannot be changed.

II — About

What happens when a million strangers each make one tiny, irreversible artistic decision?

This is the internet’s largest collaborative artwork: a single canvas created by people from all over the world, where each participant makes one tiny artistic decision and leaves one permanent mark.

There is no predetermined image and no final plan. The outcome emerges slowly through participation. Once a mark is placed, it cannot be changed, removed, corrected, or repeated.

When the final mark is placed, the artwork is complete forever. It will never change again. Each mark is equal. Each decision is final.

III — Process

How it works

The artwork is made up of exactly one million cells arranged as a single landscape-format canvas. Each participant may claim one cell and place one color into it.

Before placing a mark, each participant is asked to pause, choose a color, and confirm that they understand the permanence of the action.

The artwork is not a game, image generator, or social feed. It is a record of individual decisions made together, slowly, until the work is complete.
