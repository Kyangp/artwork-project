About the work

One Million Marks

One Million Marks is a collaborative artwork composed of one million individual decisions. Each participant contributes exactly one mark to a shared canvas.

The work has no predetermined image, composition, or outcome. It emerges gradually through participation.

Each mark is permanent. It cannot be edited, moved, or removed. There are no corrections and no second attempts.

How participation works

Before placing a mark, each participant selects a color and confirms their understanding of the permanence of the action. The placement itself is deliberate: a location is chosen, and the mark is confirmed in a single irreversible step.

Each position on the canvas can be occupied only once. Over time, the artwork fills until all one million marks have been placed.

Archival status

At present, marks are recorded locally and displayed as pending. A future archival system will publish confirmed marks as part of the canonical artwork.

When the final mark is placed and all marks are archived, the artwork will be complete. It will not change again.
